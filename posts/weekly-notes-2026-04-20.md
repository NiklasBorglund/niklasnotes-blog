## Vampire Crawlers: The Turbo Wildcard from Vampire Survivors by poncle and Nosebleed Interactive

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "243789"
}
```

From the creators of [Vampire Survivors](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/71114/vampire_survivors), we now get this dungeon crawler spin off in the same universe. You explore dungeons, build decks, and chain combos to take down monsters.

The launch seems to have been a great success. The reviews are overwhelmingly positive with around 6k reviews at the time of writing.

They clearly know how to make an addictive experience that players like. The combo mechanics and replayability get the most praise, and the concept of mashing deck-building with the Vampire Survivors seems to be appreciated. Visuals and audio gets mentions as positives too.

The deckbuilding gets some critique of being shallow and lacking depth. I guess in the midst of all other great deckbuilders, it gets tough comparisons to competitors.
Other criticisms are about players wanting more, with reviews mentioning that it is too short and too easy.

## Yunyun Syndrome!? Rhythm Psychosis by Alliance Arts and WHO YOU

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "161219"
}
```

Yunyun Syndrome!? Rhythm Psychosis is a rhythm-adventure game where you embody a hikikomori girl obsessed with denpa songs. 
I didn't know what [hikikomori](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hikikomori) or [denpa songs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denpa_song) meant before, but now I do.

Yunyun, the main character gets the main praise in the reviews where players describe her as cute and lovable. With rhythm games, the music needs to be on point and the Denpa music is often called catchy in the reviews..

The English translation of the game gets a lot of negative attention, but the developers [are addressing these issues](https://noisypixel.net/yunyun-syndrome-rhythm-psychosis-english-localization-update-1-06/) and working on fixing it already.
DLC reliance for additional content also frustrates some players.

## '83 by Blue Dot Games

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "112092"
}
```

This game was originally started by [Antimatter Games](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/developer/Antimatter%20Games), but the [studio shut down](https://www.pcgamer.com/rising-storm-studio-antimatter-games-is-being-closed/) in 2023 which left the project essentially cancelled.

Since then, Blue Dot Games, acquired the rights, got funding and [revived the project](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/1059220/view/4442331835939160310).

This Early Access launch only got mixed reviews right now, and the main culprit seems to be technical performance. Poor optimization, lag, and server instability are all mentioned.

The gunplay itself is loved by players though, feeling familiar and enjoyable. There's also clear community hope for the game's future, since the background story of how it was revived is quite compelling.

I'd assume these issues gets ironed out over the course of EA, and hopefully the review score will catch up with it.

## Flock Around by Secret Plan Games

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "237998"
}
```

Looks like a mix of PEAK and Pokémon Snap and players seem to be loving it with a near perfect score (99% positive). It's a co-op birdwatching game where you explore a nature sanctuary and photograph birds.
It's published by the publishing branch of the creators of Among Us - [Outersloth](https://www.innersloth.com/outersloth/). 

The bird photography mechanics are what players love most, and the co-op experience with friends gets mentioned a lot too.
The bird calls are apparently realistic and is much appreciated among enthusiasts. 

Not many negative comments at all so far. Some reviews mention minor performance drops from time to time. A niche game, with expectations set very clearly.

## Masters of Albion by 22cans

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "6800"
}
```

The latest game from 22cans, and [according to Peter Molyneux himself](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4glw5nyrggo) - his final game. It is a God game and city builder where you shape the world of Albion. 
It launched with mixed reviews in Early Access, and it seems to be a mix of frustration with certain mechanics and built up expectations not met.

Players love the nostalgic feel of the game, often referencing Fable and Black & White. The visual style gets appreciated, and there's community hope for future improvements.

Performance issues with lag and optimization are the strongest complaint. The god powers feel limited and without real impact, building mechanics frustrate with placement restrictions, and there seems to be a cooking minigame mechanics dominate gameplay in a way many find tedious.

The game have a lot of charm according to many, but at the moment the nostalgia seems to be doing more work than the game is. Will be interesting to see how this evolves during EA.
