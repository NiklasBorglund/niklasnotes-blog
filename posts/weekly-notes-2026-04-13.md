PRAGMATA tops the charts with one of the fastest-selling launches we've seen in a while. 
Windrose converts its massive wishlist into a solid debut. And MOUSE: P.I. For Hire proves that the 1930s cartoon aesthetic still has plenty of life in it.

Let's check what happened last week!

---

## PRAGMATA by CAPCOM CO., Ltd.

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "208842"
}
```

This week's smash hit was first announced in 2020 in the PS5 reveal lineup as [CAPCOM's first original franchise in 8 years](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pragmata). 
After years of development and delays it finally launched and sold over [1 million copies in just 2 days!](https://www.polygon.com/pragmata-launch-sales-single-player-games/). At the time of writing it has over 20k reviews and a score of 97% overwhelmingly positive worldwide.

In the game, you control Hugh and an android named Diana navigating a rogue AI-controlled lunar facility. 
The relationship between Hugh and Diana is what players talk about most, often described as heartwarming and wholesome.

Visuals and art direction also get lots of praise, and the performance is notably well optimized (it's even deck verified). The negatives are minor in comparison. A small percentage feel the narrative is a bit thin, and game length and value come up as concerns for some players.
Denuvo is the strongest specific complaint, with Linux players running into access issues.

A clear hit for Capcom.

## Windrose by Kraken Express

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "193654"
}
```

Open world survival crafting game set in an alternative Age of Piracy. You captain a ship, gather a crew, explore procedurally generated biomes, and take on legendary foes across dungeons and quests. Published by Pocketpair.

They had one of the [top demos](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/258792/windrose_demo) in the latest next fest and managed to gather over [1.5 million wishlist](https://playwindrose.com/1500000-wishlists-thank-you/) up until this launch.

Quality of life features get the strongest praise, with players appreciating how crafting and inventory management feels compared to other games in the genre. Combat mechanics are the main complaint, described as clunky and unresponsive.

The Chinese audience is notably more negative in score than other regions, while most other markets sit comfortably in the high eighties and nineties. I did a quick custom report, and it seems like they have the same complaints but judge them much harder.

## MOUSE: P.I. For Hire by Fumi Games

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "61050"
}
```

The classic 1928 Mickey Mouse short film Steamboat Willie (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steamboat_Willie) entered the public domain in 2024, helping bring renewed attention to early black-and-white cartoon aesthetics. Fast forward to 2026, and we get creations like this. 
Looks fantastic, and placed in a noir world. 

You play as a detective mouse in the town of Mouseburg, shooting your way through corruption and crime. Overwhelmingly Positive with more than 5k reviews, and the art style is clearly the star.

Players love the visuals and animation, and voice acting gets called out specifically, with [Troy Baker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troy_Baker) among the cast.

Not many negatives. Some technical issues with bugs and crashes, enemy variety feels slightly thin across levels, and difficulty balance divides players.

## BidKing by MindSurge Network & Games

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "239444"
}
```

Multiplayer auction game where players bluff, act, and use special abilities to outbid opponents and collect loot. Launched with Mostly Negative reviews, which is unusually low for a game pulling in over 5k reviews, especially at a price point of just 2.55 EUR.

Server crashes and maintenance issues are the biggest complaint by far, making the game frustrating to actually play. Second to that is the pay-to-win monetization that got massive critique.
Players also critique the use of AI-generated character designs and assets, which brings down the overall feel of quality for the game.

The auction mechanics themselves get mixed feedback. Some find the concept interesting, but balance and depth concerns keep it from really landing.

The review volume suggests the concept got real attention, even if the launch clearly didn’t hold up.

## REPLACED by Sad Cat Studios

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "47724"
}
```

Another game that had a [successful demo](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/258815/replaced_demo) in the latest next fest. Published by Swedish [Thunderful Publishing](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/publisher/Thunderful%20Publishing). 

It's a 2.5D action platformer set in a dystopian cyberpunk world. Global score is mostly positive, where most audiences giving it around 80%+, with the exception of Simplified Chinese with around 60% positive score.

The visuals are clearly what people are talking about the most. The pixel art and overall aesthetic is gorgeous, and the soundtrack adds to the atmosphere.

The platforming itself is a big source of frustration, with controls and difficulty getting called out in the reviews. 
Combat gets mixed reactions, and some players are disappointed by the lack of voice acting given the narrative ambitions.

## Early Access Graduates
