The Weeping Swan carries the weight of a smash hit predecessor. RACCOIN makes coin pushing genuinely strategic. Cinderia launches strong as a dark fairytale roguelite. And Hozy is exactly as cozy as it sounds.

Let's check them out!

---

## The Weeping Swan: Ten Days of the City's Fall by 零创游戏(ZerocreationGame)

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "76543"
}
```

Game that got most attention in terms of reviews this week was The Weeping Swan. Psychological horror visual novel set during the fall of the Ming Dynasty and the audience is overwhelmingly chinese.
These games seem to generally be popular among them, but this one had a rougher launch. Their previous game, [The Hungry Lamb](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/57739/the_hungry_lamb_traveling_in_the_late_ming_dynasty), was a smash hit. 

So I imagine the game had quite a bit of hype surrounding it.

Character development gets praise, particularly a character called Lin Bianbian who players find sympathetic. Visual and audio quality get mixed reactions where some love the art style, but others find inconsistencies.

However, the narrative structure seems to be the main issue, with a dual past/present timeline that some find disjointed and hard to follow.

## RACCOIN: Coin Pusher Roguelike by Doraccoon

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "224588"
}
```

After a [very popular demo released last year](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/240934/raccoin_coin_pusher_roguelike_demo), RACCOIN launched with not quite as high, but still a solid Very Positive score. 

The gameplay loop is addictive with players losing track of time, and the coin pusher mechanics feel satisfying as coins interact and cascade. 

The negatives are mostly about RNG with progression that feels too luck-dependent for some, which undercuts the strategic side. Shop mechanics and round length also draw criticism.

## Subliminal by Accidental Studios

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "54506"
}
```

First-person psychological horror where you manipulate light to solve puzzles and uncover memories. Mixed overall, but seem to have gotten quite a lot of attention from a demo released last year.

As usual with horror games, visual and audio design are the clear highlight in the positive reviews. Developer engagement gets positive mentions too.

The negatives are critiquing some of the core gameplay elements. Puzzles being overly complex without clear guidance, chase sequences poorly designed and punishing, controls feeling janky and unresponsive. 

Players also feel misled by promotional material and what they tried on the demo. The expectations vs reality gap is showing up in the score.

---

## Hozy by Come On Studio

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "103325"
}
```

Hozy is a cozy cleaning and decorating game about returning to your faded hometown and restoring it room by room. 
The setup is aimed at a low stress vibe. When I look at the store page it's very cleary written with setting the expectations early. The very first sentence on the page is - "Welcome Home". 

That seems to have landed well, because the visuals and overall atmosphere get a lot of praise and players find the cleaning and furnishing loop satisfying.

The biggest complaint is not really about the concept, but about how much of it there is. 
The game has nine unique locations, where many say it feels too short for the price. 

Players seem to like Hozy overall, they just want more of it.

## Cinderia by MyACG Studio

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "187511"
}
```

Cinderia is a dark fairytale action roguelite. Great launch with very positive sentiment overall.

The strongest praise is around character variety, build variety, and the overall art style. 

Different playable characters appear to give the game real replay value instead of just superficial variety, which matters a lot in this kind of roguelite. 

The negatives are more typical Early Access issues, mainly technical problems and some localization complaints.
