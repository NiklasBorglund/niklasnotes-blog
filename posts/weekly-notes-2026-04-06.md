A week where wishlists and demo numbers told only part of the story. 
Solo developed Road to Vostok had 800K demo players and delivered. 

Crystalfall had 100K wishlists and launched to one of the lowest scores we've seen in a while. 
Samson released with many reviewers saying it lacks polish and overall finish on specific features, which might be related to the studio's layoffs last year.

Let's dive in!

---

## Road to Vostok by Road to Vostok Ltd.

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "4210"
}
```

Road to Vostok is a solo developed game by Finnish developer Antti Leinonen. It's a single-player survival game set in a post-apocalyptic border zone between Finland and Russia.
It had over 800k players on the demo and managed to build up a serious amount of hype. His dedication to the game and transparency towards the community is mentioned a lot in the reviews.

And it seems to have paid off. It has already [sold over 200k units](https://www.youtube.com/post/Ugkxn6iK_4CTuzHYnNWmPuXHjxT-APvS_tPH) and got 82% positive with more than 5k reviews.

Players specifically call out the optimization, atmosphere and immersion.

On the negative side, AI enemy behavior is the biggest complaint where enemies reportedly spot and shoot from unrealistic distances. 
Crafting and looting mechanics get mixed reactions, and map design is criticized for lacking depth and variety.

## Samson by Liquid Swords

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "245579"
}
```

The studio that made Samson, Liquid Swords, was founded by Christofer Sundberg - a co-founder of [Avalanche Studios](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/developer/Avalanche%20Studios).
Unfortunately, they had a [round of layoffs last year](https://www.liquidswords.com/news/liquid-swords-announces-proposed-workforce-reduction-amid-business-challenges) which likely affected the development and adds useful context to its launch.

It had a rough launch week, with only around 50% positive globally. You play as the character Samson, fighting and driving your way out of debt through an open world with choices that affect the story.

City design, atmosphere and the immersive world all get praise, but the negatives are quite dominant. All of them revolve around lack of polish and an unfinished feel to many features.
Combat mechanics are the strongest complaint with clunky and unresponsive melee. Driving mechanics frustrates with poor handling and unrealistic vehicle damage. Technical issues pile on with bugs and performance problems.

## Crystalfall by CRG Studio

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "153539"
}
```

This is a free-to-play online action RPG in a steampunk post-apocalyptic setting. They got over 100k wishlists before launch according to a [post on their website](https://crystalfall.com/news/69a9a3cdd639377ad5ad1af7).

After following new releases for a while now, I feel like F2P games generally get a lower rating since there is no cost of entry, but Crystalfall is currently sitting at only 22% positive with around 2k reviews.
They [released a demo](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/258836/crystalfall_demo) during next fest that got a higher, but still quite low with 58%. 

The overwhelmingly top negative mention is the server issues. A lot of players can't simply play, which explains the score. Other things are microtransaction pricing piles on, with inventory space and stash-related monetization drawing criticism. 
Performance and gameplay mechanics add further frustration.

I guess they are hard at work trying to fix these issues and some players show patience given the Early Access status, but there is not much positive momentum cutting through yet.

## Morbid Metal by SCREEN JUICE

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "153620"
}
```

Fast-paced hack-and-slash set in a dystopian future published by Ubisoft. It launched in Early Access with very positive reviews at the moment. Combat fluidity is what players talk about most, praising it as smooth and responsive.
The visuals and soundtrack also get consistent praise.

Crashes and performance issues are the main complaints, and the amount of upgrades for the characters feels a bit thin for some players. 
Difficulty is a split topic, punishing enough that some bounce off it, though others see that as exactly the point.

## The Spotter: Dig or Die by Puppyroar Games

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "235414"
}
```

First-person survival where you dig for resources during the day and defend against mutant hordes at night. The core gameplay loop is clearly what's winning people over where people note it to be highly addictive.
Developer engagement with the community also gets called out specifically, which is always a good sign.

The stamina system and resource management frustrate some players, particularly the need to constantly head back for supplies. Upgrade system gets mixed reactions, and technical issues with crashes show up as well on the negative side.
