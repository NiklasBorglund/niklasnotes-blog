## Crimson Desert by Pearl Abyss

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "111578"
}
```

One of the most anticipated releases this year, with [over 3 million(!)](https://x.com/CrimsonDesert_/status/2028832772568093140) wishlists, Crimson Desert launched to a "Mostly Positive" global score.

The South Korean developer, Pearl Abyss, announced it 6 years ago and it was originally intended to be a prequel to their game [Black Desert](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/4222/black_desert) - but overtime it grew into its own thing.
And with a hype this big and a development time this long, it's difficult to manage player expectations while still have creative control over your game. The developers themselves even admitted that they [announced the game too early in a recent interview](https://www.polygon.com/crimson-desert-announced-too-early/).

And as you can see below from the playtime distribution chart, the players who play the game the least amount of time give the game significantly lower score.
I've seen graphs that looks like this on the site many times now, and to me, it's a clear sign that players expected something else than what they bought. Otherwise the negatives would be more spread out.

![Crimson Desert Score Distribution](./score-distribution-crimson.png)

The game's graphical performance and world design get high praise with players saying its visually impressive, especially if you play on high setting.
Controls are the dominant complaint, described as clunky and unintuitive. Story and narrative quality disappoint many as well as visual clarity issues make it look blurry to some (contrary to the visual praise from others).

The regional split is notable, Brazilian Portuguese players are the most positive at 90%, while Chinese Simplified and Korean players both sit at 60% Mixed.
Most western regions hover around 80-81%.

## DEATH STRANDING 2: ON THE BEACH by Nixxes Software & KOJIMA PRODUCTIONS

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "257309"
}
```

Kojima's follow-up to his delivery-across-a-post-apocalyptic-landscape game hits Steam - and the players really love it with 96% overwhelmingly positive.

Visuals and atmosphere lead the praise, with gameplay improvements over the first game and performance optimization on lower-end systems both getting mentions. 
Narrative depth and emotional impact round it out as is usually a strong point for Kojima games.

The negatives are mostly technical, Linux compatibility issues, HDR not working, G-Sync flickering, frame generation problems. 

Overall a successful launch and port to PC.

## The Seven Deadly Sins: Origin by Netmarble F&C

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "207707"
}
```

A free to play open-world action RPG based on [the anime/manga series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Seven_Deadly_Sins_(manga) with the same name. It had a rough launch, with only 52% positive.

The positives are not that many. Some mention visual appeal, community engagement, and story engagement for existing fans.

Technical optimization is the dominant complaint. The game is visually impressive, but it does not run well for many. Gacha mechanics get heavy criticism for being expensive and unbalanced. Movement feels janky with input lag for many.

Generally, the entry point to free to play games is well... free. And they often get judged by their monetization reception if it feels fair or not, and this one is not off to a good start on that front.

## Retro Rewind - Video Store Simulator by Blood Pact Studios

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "207182"
}
```

Once every now and then, we get a new realistic simulator game that hits the sweet spot for a lot of people. 
Retro Rewind lets you run a classic video rental store and tries to maximize the nostalgia of the player in doing so.

With an overwhelmingly positive score, nostalgia is the dominant theme with players connecting emotionally with the 90s video store atmosphere. 
A lot of players also enjoy the high involvement of the developers in the community.

Many express concerns regarding gameplay depth, indicating a desire for more engaging mechanics and features.

## Scritchy Scratchy by Lunch Money Games

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "227947"
}
```

Simple concept, surprisingly addictive. Scritchy Scratchy is a scratch card incremental game that had a very successful launch.

Players absolutely love the gameplay loop, where they report to play longer than they initially intended. The median playtime among reviewers is about 10 hours. 

The unique scratch-off theme feels fresh, and accessibility options for reducing wrist strain are a nice touch that gets called out positively by a few.

Some feel the content runs thin after the initial excitement, and the prestige/achievement system receives mixed signals.
