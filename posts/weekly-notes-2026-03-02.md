Two massive launches dropped on the same day this week - Slay the Spire 2 and Marathon. One is a sequel to one of the most beloved deckbuilders ever made, the other a revival of Bungie's classic 90s shooter series. 
Alongside those, a chaotic WWI shooter, a solo-dev CRPG, and a new Songs of Conquest DLC from Lavapotion where I work.

Let's dive in!

---

## Slay the Spire 2 by Mega Crit

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "63357"
}
```

I haven't played it myself, but I don't think I've ever met anyone who played the first [Slay the Spire](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/63356/slay_the_spire) and not loved it.
With this extreme hype leading up to launch, it was no surprise that it would be topping the charts this week.

Players are loving it, already at a 96% overwhelmingly positive and over 40k reviews globally. The visual improvements are appreciated, but some players feel like its too similar to the first game with a lot of recycled content.
But there are new characters, new mechanics, and a lot of content that enabled a lot of replayability which is noted and praised by many.

It also introduces a co-op mode, so you can play with a friend.

On the criticism side is mainly balancing issues. Quite a few reviews thinks that it can feel overly punishing at times - something I'd assume they will work on during Early Access.

## Marathon by Bungie

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "160319"
}
```

The very same day as Slay the Spire 2, Bungie released Marathon. A revival of their early game series for Mac OS, released back in the early 90s. You can play the [classic ones for free on Steam](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/107759/classic_marathon) as they have been revived for modern hardware by the fan community.

I always liked their games, I've had great fun with both the Halo series and the Destiny games. 
The development of Marathon however, has not been entirely free from controversy. Last year, they were in a [plagiarism scandal](https://www.forbes.com/sites/paultassi/2025/12/03/bungies-marathon-plagiarism-scandal-comes-to-a-satisfactory-end/) that has since been resolved. I wonder if this had any impact on the sales.

The players that bought it seems to enjoy it. It has a very positive score globally with almost 30k reviews at the time of writing.

Tactical combat in teams is the standout positive with players finding it exhilarating. Great Gunplay is something I always liked in their previous titles, and it's getting praise for feel and responsiveness here too. 
The art style is frequently called out as distinctive.

Solo play is a strongly negative topic where the experience significantly suffers without a team, which is a real barrier for a lot of players. 
Monetization draws fire too, with cosmetics and premium content pricing coming up repeatedly. Server performance and matchmaking issues round out the concerns at launch.

The Chinese audience gives it a much lower score at 60% Mixed while English, French and German players are all 90-91% -a notable gap worth watching.

## Over The Top: WWI by Flying Squirrel Entertainment

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "21243"
}
```

Massively multiplayer WWI action with fully destructible environments and chaotic trench warfare. 
Players love the trench digging mechanics that persist between matches, adding a nice strategic layer.

Gameplay chaos is a strong positive too with the unpredictability of battles is a feature. 

On the negative side, hit registration frustrates players, graphics and performance get mixed reactions, and UI/controls need work.

## Esoteric Ebb by Christoffer Bodegård

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "122812"
}
```

Solo developer, published by [Raw Fury](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/publisher/Raw%20Fury). Raw Fury had a few hits lately (like [ROUTINE](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/176880/routine), [Monsters are Coming! Rock & Road](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/208617/monsters_are_coming_rock_road), [The Séance of Blake Manor](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/133621/the_s%C3%A9ance_of_blake_manor)), and this one can be added to the mix.

Narrative engagement leads the praise, with humor and art style close behind. The [Disco Elysium](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/138583/disco_elysium_the_final_cut) comparison shows up repeatedly in reviews - which is high praise but also sets expectations.

The main criticisms are dialogue density causing fatigue, lore dumps disrupting flow, and character development feeling a bit shallow. Political themes also get called heavy-handed by some.

## Songs of Conquest - Rise Eternal by Lavapotion

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "153"
}
```

We had a release again from Lavapotion this week! This time our latest DLC for [Songs of Conquest](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/149/songs_of_conquest) - one that is focused on story maps.
It has similar length to one of the campaigns in the base game and consists of four maps where you control the undead wielder Kastus Maal.

I'm glad this one has finally been released, we've had it in the pipeline for a long time. My colleague and I even went to GamesCom back in 2024 to promote it along with the console version - but we had to postpone it at the time.

And players like it a lot! The story is well liked, the new risen/necromancy mechanics are appreciated as well as the price point of the DLC. 
A lot of players have been wanting more story content for a while as it's their primary mode to play the game, and it's nice to see that it is received well.

The next DLC for songs is named [Yulan](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/255406/songs_of_conquest_yulan), which brings another full faction to the game.
