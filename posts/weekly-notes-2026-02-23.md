## Resident Evil Requiem by CAPCOM CO., Ltd.

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "209375"
}
```

Apart from next fest, we had a massive release from Capcom. ~100k reviews in just over a week is selling the copies at an incredible speed. Not many games last year (or even previous RE games) had that type of launch velocity.

It launched with an impressive 96% Overwhelmingly Positive and the atmosphere, visual quality in the graphics, emotional and excellent story telling all get plenty of praise.

However, players express frustration over the low field of view (FOV) setting, which causes discomfort and motion sickness. Controlling the character Grace often gets mentioned as slow and tedious gameplay wise - and some technical issues in general are also noted among players.

Last Resident Evil game I played was [Revelations 2](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/146398/resident_evil_revelations_2) in co-op (brilliant game btw), but Requiem here makes me want try this one out.

# Steam Next Fest

The other big event last week was Steam Next Fest. Tons of developers showing off their creations and gathering wishlists for the releases of their game.

It's an exciting week, and this time I even had a game participating myself through Lavapotion!

## Wielders of the Essence Demo by Lavapotion

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "258725"
}
```

First up is the game I worked on for the last 6 months or so. After 9+ years of working on Songs of Conquest, it's so much fun to work on something in the beginning stages of a project again.
Thinking about what makes the game fun, rapidly iterating and finding the core of the experience.

We call it an "incremental base-building horde survival" and its very inspired by games like [Vampire Survivors](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/71114/vampire_survivors), [Halls of Torment](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/49959/halls_of_torment), [Megabonk](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/64937/megabonk), but with elements from incremental games and base building and the universe from Songs of Conquest.

Some of the unwritten rules of the genre of the game have been broken in this game, on purpose because we think it gives a fun and unique addition to the genre. But in doing that, we have to be really good at managing expectations.
In store pages, trailers, and even in game we have been adding the message to the players to have them know BEFORE they start playing that, this is the game you are playing - our game.

Players liked the potential of the game, the base building, the incremental meta progression - when reading the steam reviews. Some disliked the combat mechanics, early game progression, and that they wanted more content.

We landed on 85% positive, got a lot of attention from youtubers and streamers - and a bunch of really good feedback. So I'd say we had a successful next fest.

## Windrose Demo by Windrose Crew

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "258792"
}
```

The day before next fest started, Windrose announced that they had amassed over [1 million wishlists](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/3041230/view/498346952550778886).

Impressive, and with those numbers there is no surprise that it was one of the demos that got the most reviews during this run. At the time of writing it had 7k with 93% positive.

It's a pirate survival sandbox where you build, sail the seas and get blown up by cannonballs. Naval combat is liked a lot, with players comparing it favorably to other pirate games.
The onboarding is praised for getting you up to speed quickly without having to spend much time in menus and learning the game.

The main friction points are co-op where players want to share ships and resources but can't - and resource gathering feeling a bit grindy for crafting and upgrades. 
Combat system gets mixed signals, some love it, some find it clunky.

## Far Far West Demo by Evil Raptor

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "258804"
}
```
Robot cowboys hunting supernatural threats in a bizarre Wild West. Almost a perfect score (98%) and around 6k reviews.

Gameplay mechanics lead the praise, specifically spell combos and movement. The art style is charming and distinctive, co-op feels well-executed with shared objectives, and the soundtrack gets frequent shoutouts.

Negatives are light given the score with some performance issues with frame rates, and enemy AI pathfinding/targeting needs work according to some.

A highly successful demo, which they now have taken down to prepare for the full release I assume. They wrote a celebratory post on steam about [their Next Fest experience](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/3124540/view/528747518463311927).

## Outbound Demo by Square Glade Games

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "258802"
}
```

Another Demo coming into next fest [with over a million wishlists](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/2681030/view/509595167628462780). Highly anticipated cozy open-world road trip where you  customize a camper van, build, farm, and explore colorful biomes.

The cozy atmosphere is the clear hook, with the soundtrack and visuals getting praise - and I agree, it looks absolutely gorgeous. Building mechanics around the mobile home are appreciated, and multiplayer generates genuine excitement.

The negatives are more substantive though — resource management frustrates players (gathering limits in particular), vehicle handling feels sluggish, and the gameplay loop gets called repetitive. No storyline is a disappointment for some.

A solid score, but I can imagine they had hoped for more reviews and more praise with that amount of wishlists going in.
