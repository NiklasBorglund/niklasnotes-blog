Far Far West sells half a million units in days and quickly becomes one of the biggest launches of the month. Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era launched in Early Access. And two more "PEAK-likes".

Let's have a look!

---

## Far Far West by Evil Raptor

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "209521"
}
```

There's been a few wildly popular extraction shooters released lately, and this is the latest one. Feels like a genre that is very much on a high note right now.
It's set in a form of Wild West where you play as robot cowboys.

It [sold over 500k units](https://www.enadglobal7.com/press-releases/far-far-west-delivers-strong-initial-sales-on-steam-with-over-500000-units-sold-the-first-days-after-launch/) in just a couple of days.
Overwhelmingly Positive in Early Access with over 22K reviews, made it quickly rise to one of the most popular releases of the month.

Players love the spell mechanics most, along with the visual style and sheer replayability. The community is excited of what the game could become after more updates.
The gunplay is a complaint made by many, which I feel is unusual given the genre. The game world gets called empty by some, enemy design repetitive, and co-op mechanics don't always feel cooperative. 

But with 96% positive already, I'd guess those issues are seen as minor by the majority.

---

## Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era by Unfrozen

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "44639"
}
```

People have been craving a new form of the HoMM 3 experience ever since it was first released back in 1999. None of the games that came after really scratched that itch.
And when we started developing [Songs of Conquest](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/149/songs_of_conquest) back in 2017, one of our goals was to help revive this genre - so I'm genuinely happy to see this land well.

This is the eighth entry in the HoMM series, developed by Unfrozen and published by both Ubisoft and Hooded Horse.
Very Positive closing in on 90%, and nostalgia is mentioned the most in the reviews. Players feel it captures the essence of HoMM 3, and the strategic depth and campaign story gets alot of praise. 
Developer responsiveness to feedback is also called out, which matters a lot in Early Access.

The AI difficulty balancing is the biggest complaint, with players reporting the AI effectively cheats. It's a tricky problem, we've had the same complaints in our game.
Multiplayer has connectivity issues, and the visual style divides people, with some finding it a bit bland or cartoonish.

## Gamble With Your Friends by Team GWYF

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "224682"
}
```

Another hit from Sweden, this time from Stockholm. Already [sold over 1 million copies](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/3892270/view/701015907648931195?l=english).
It's a co-op multiplayer game where you and friends gamble your way through a casino to pay off a shared debt.

The co-op and the dynamic friend interaction is the main draw of the game, same as most of these type of PEAK-likes (I'm coining this term now).

Players love the humor, and that it knowingly leans into the problems with gambling addictions. The visual and audio design get appreciation too.

The negatives center around the game feeling a bit thin over time. Some say gameplay variety lacks depth and replayability, the strict time limits frustrate some, and the daily quota system adds pressure that not everyone enjoys.

## s&box by Facepunch Studios

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "103414"
}
```

The developers of both [Garry's Mod](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/4342/garry_s_mod) and [Rust](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/29769/rust) launched their spiritual successor to Garry's Mod.
It's a game creation platform built on Valve's Source 2 engine. 

With the insanely impressive track record of their previous games, also comes sky-high expectations. s&box launched with only mixed reviews.
Performance is the biggest issue with crashes, low FPS and poor optimization dominating the negative reviews. 
AI-generated content flooding the platform is a strong secondary complaint, with players feeling it hurts the overall quality and creativity of what's available. 

The cosmetic system also gets called overly monetized.

However, a lot of players see lots of potential in the game, and given the weight Facepunch's earlier titles - they might very well be able to turn it around.

## Sledding Game by The Sledding Corporation

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "149177"
}
```

Multiplayer sledding (PEAK-like?) game that launches after [a very successful demo](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/232325/sledding_game_demo) that was a participant in Steam Next Fest. Launches with an impressive 97% positive globally

I talk about managing expectations a lot on this blog, and I think usually it's the calmer more niche games that manages to do it the best.
You sled, socialize, and mess around in various winter activities. Snowball fights, curling, fishing, ragdoll physics, character customization. You quickly get what its about.

It sold over [100k copies](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/3438850/view/701015907648929793) in just a few days.
Community interaction and the cozy atmosphere are what players love most, and the sledding mechanics themselves get called fun and engaging. Minigames also adds to it.

Some technical issues with physics and save file problems, and players want crossplay between platforms. A few also want more structured gameplay objectives.
