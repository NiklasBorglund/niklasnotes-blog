John Carpenter's Toxic Commando brings co-op zombie chaos. Monster Hunter Stories 3 tops the charts but gets overshadowed by crash reports. Fatal Frame II returns with a faithful remake. A solo dev surreal open world quietly earns 94%. And WWE 2K26 continues its complicated relationship with PC players.

Let's dive in!

## John Carpenter's Toxic Commando by Saber Interactive

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "225452"
}
```

Co-op horde shooter with zombies, vehicular combat, and [John Carpenter's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Carpenter) name on the box. 

Globally it gets a 77% score (Mixed), but it's mainly being pulled down by Chinese reviews giving it only around 42-43% positive. Most other audiences gives it 80%+. 

Gunplay is the most positive theme among the reviews with solid weight and feel to the weapons, and vehicle mechanics add a fun layer to the gameplay. 
Graphics are well appreciated too.

The top negative is about performance, issues with crashes and FPS drops are the biggest concern (also seems to be the main criticism among the negative simplified chinese reviews).
Other things are that missions feel repetitive, story gets called bland by some, and character design feeling off.

## Monster Hunter Stories 3: Twisted Reflection by CAPCOM CO., Ltd.

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "220630"
}
```

The sequel to [Monster Hunter Stories 2: Wings of Ruin](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/19270/monster_hunter_stories_2_wings_of_ruin) which is a turn-based creature collector JRPG.
You raise and bond with monsters, explore an open world and get to do all of that in an emotional story. It's mostly positive across the board, with review scores landing between 60-80%.

Graphics and character development get lots of praise, and the monster management systems add strategic depth according to many.

But game crashes are dominating the reviews and is by far the most mentioned - brings to mind last year's smash hit [Monster Hunter Wilds](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/151952/monster_hunter_wilds) that got massive critique on performance. 
There's an "anti-tamper" system from Denuvo that players also express frustration about, and players note a lack of endgame content.

## FATAL FRAME II: Crimson Butterfly REMAKE by KOEI TECMO GAMES CO., LTD.

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "230342"
}
```

This one is a remake of a classic survival horror [game released back in 2003 on PS2.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatal_Frame_II:_Crimson_Butterfly) You play as twin sisters lost in a haunted village.

Visual quality leads the praise with many improvements from the original, with the atmospheric storytelling staying faithful. Fans of the series seem happy in general.

The friction points are combat difficulty and new mechanics. New enemy health and stamina systems frustrate players. Technical performance issues add to it with frame rate locks and drops.

## Lucid Blocks by Lucy B. Locks

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "196741"
}
```

Love the look of this game. Unique and surreal first-person open world with procedural generation, crafting, and Lovecraftian vibes. 

From what I can tell, it's made by a solo developer and already got 94% positive and closing in on 2k reviews. Strong result for a game of this size.

Exploration and discovery lead the praise, with players loving the vastness of the world and the sense of wonder it creates. 
Atmospheric design and the surreal gameplay experience round it out, and this one clearly nails that part of the experience.

Not many negatives. Some technical issues, enemy design feeling overwhelming, and gameplay variety concerns.

## Solasta II by Tactical Adventures

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "144987"
}
```
Tactical party-based CRPG in Early Access, sequel to [Solasta](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/133318/solasta_crown_of_the_magister).

Gameplay mechanics and combat get genuine praise, carrying over the tactical fun from the first game. Visual quality is improved and voice acting is appreciated.

Character creation gets the most criticism, with many players noting limited options for distinguishing male and female characters and finding the default faces fall into uncanny valley territory.
Clearly an issue that drives the negative reviews and mentioned alot.

Technical issues with crashes and bugs add to it. I assume some of these issues will be ironed out during Early Access. 75% positive is not the best, but it's entirely possible to get back from.

## WWE 2K26 by Visual Concepts

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "255519"
}
```

The WWE 2K series seems to have a complicated relationship with its PC player base, and this entry continues that trend with 46% positive at the time of writing.

Previous sport [games entries from Visual Concepts](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/developer/Visual%20Concepts) are all quite low in positive sentiment scores, typically ranging between 40-60 percent.
However, the [meta critic score is much higher at 79](https://www.metacritic.com/game/wwe-2k26/) - which makes me think they mainly target a console audience instead of PC. Still, the critic vs audience split in opinion here is interesting.

Anyway, gameplay mechanics and character customization get genuine praise, and players who enjoy it really do put the hours in. 

The Ringside Pass system is the dominant complaint at ~22% which requires excessive grinding to unlock content. Microtransactions pile on at ~9%, character unlocking feels grindy, and technical stability issues with crashes add to it.
