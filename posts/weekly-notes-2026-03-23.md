## Life is Strange: Reunion by Deck Nine Games

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "252805"
}
```

The Life is Strange series returns with Reunion, a narrative adventure featuring time manipulation and emotional storytelling. 
Published by Square Enix, developed by Deck Nine who have been handling the series for a while now.

This appears to be the conclusion of a story that began with the original [Life is Strange](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/156171/life_is_strange_episode_1). That game’s ending split the fanbase, forcing players into a final choice many never fully accepted. 
Reunion picks a side in that divide, which feels like long-awaited closure to some fans and like a betrayal of the original to others.

Kotaku went as far as to call it a ["Fix Fic"](https://kotaku.com/life-is-strange-reunion-the-kotaku-review-2000683662), arguing that it undermines the original game’s intent.

Looking at the reviews, nostalgia and emotional impact lead the praise, with fans happy to see beloved returning characters. The rewind mechanic integrates well into the story according to most.

On the negative side, technical issues keep coming up, with bugs and crashes mentioned by several reviewers.

Most reviewers seem to actually complete the game with the 10% percentile of playtime being as high as 7.5H.

## EverSiege: Untold Ages by Tindalos Interactive

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "205492"
}
```

This is a hero-driven strategy roguelite from Tindalos Interactive that mixes tower defense, RTS, and action into a pretty unusual hybrid. Solo or co-op up to three players. Mostly Positive overall.

Many praise the gameplay loop, replayability, and the way its different genre pieces actually blend.
The biggest complaints are on the technical side, with bugs and performance issues showing up often, while co-op gets extra criticism because only the host keeps progression

## Nova Roma by Lion Shield

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "126982"
}
```

Published by Hooded Horse, this Roman city builder launched in Early Access with a fantastic start. 96% Overwhelmingly Positive. Must say, looks pretty good - it got a wishlist from me.

Water physics mechanics are the thing that is mentioned in the reviews the most. The charming visuals add to the appeal. The gods system in the game adds an interesting layer of complexity to city management.

Negatives are typical Early Access issues - citizen AI behavior needs work, aqueduct construction feels cumbersome, and some feel the price is a bit high for the current content amount. 

Players seem (obviously, considering the score) optimistic about where it's headed though.

## Way of the Hunter 2 by Nine Rocks Games

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "247356"
}
```

Open-world hunting simulation in Early Access. Published by THQ Nordic.

Graphical quality is the clear positive, looking noticeably better than [the first game](https://niklasnotes.com/dashboard/game/133926/way_of_the_hunter). Animal tracking mechanics and developer community engagement also get praise.

Technical issues are the largest complaint though with bugs, crashes, and performance problems that are widespread. Animal AI behavior affects hunting realism, and hunting mechanics themselves get questioned for weapon effectiveness.

Lots of players seem to be understanding that it's an Early Access title, but highlight the need for the technical issues being fixed as soon as possible.

## Mega Man Star Force Legacy Collection by CAPCOM CO., Ltd.

```condensedgamecard
{
"gameId": "237806"
}
```

Mega Man Star Force Legacy Collection is a remastered package of the Nintendo DS-era Star Force games. The fans love it. All seven games from the series in one collection, with enhanced graphics, quality of life improvements, and online features.
Quality of life improvements are the clearest positive, with players appreciating things like autosave, encounter-rate options, and other tweaks that make the old games much easier to revisit today.

Nostalgia is obviously a strong factor too, with many happy just to see this part of Mega Man history brought back.
